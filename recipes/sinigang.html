Sinigang

Description

Pork sinigang is another classic Filipino cuisine distinguished by its distinct sour flavour palette - good for cold, rainy weather to provide that warmth and kick from the tamarind to keep you going throughout the day

Ingredients

  • 250 g pork belly
  • 1 bunch spinach
  • three tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 bunch string beans, cut in 1 inch length
  • 2 eggplants, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 piece onion, diced
  • half of garlic bulb, diced
  • a pack of sinigang mix

Steps

  1. Heat cooking oil in pot
  2. Add onion and garlic until slightly browned
  3. Add pork belly and cook until slightly browned
  4. Add fish sauce and two cups of water. Bring to a boil
  5. Add tomatoes and let simmer for 40 minutes or until pork is tender
  6. Add sinigang mix and all vegetables except spinach into pot. Let simmer for 5 minutes
  7. Turn off heat and add spinach into pot. Cover and let residual heat cook the spinach for 5 minutes
  8. Serve with freshly cooked rice
